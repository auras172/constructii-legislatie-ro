## Heading/count ambiguity — resolution (2026-06-29)

**Problem:** The Markdown file contains 44 headings at level `###` but `article_count` in metadata is 43. This discrepancy confused heading-based parsers and RAG systems that counted structural headings rather than semantic articles.

**Root cause:** `Articolul I` of HG 343/2017 is a modifying article that rewrites HG 273/1994. Its body includes the replacement text for `art. 1` of HG 273/1994, rendered as:

```markdown
### Articolul I {#art-i}

Hotărârea Guvernului nr. 273/1994 ... se modifică după cum urmează:
... 2. Articolul 1 va avea următorul cuprins:

### Articolul 1 {#art-1}

Se aprobă Regulamentul privind recepția construcțiilor ...
```

The inner `### Articolul 1 {#art-1}` is **not** an independent HG 343/2017 article — it is replacement text for an article of HG 273/1994, embedded inside the body of `Articolul I`. This is standard legislative technique for a modifying act ("act modificator").

**Why heading levels were not changed:** The nested heading falls inside the `<!-- OFFICIAL_TEXT_START -->` / `<!-- OFFICIAL_TEXT_END -->` block. That block is immutable official text; changing heading levels would alter the structural representation of the official document.

**Resolution chosen — documentation only:**
1. `metadata/acts/hg-343-2017.json` — added `heading_count_note` field explaining the discrepancy; `article_count` stays 43.
2. `import-log/2026-06-28-hg-343-2017.md` (this file) — this section documents the ambiguity and decision.
3. `docs/anchor-convention.md` — added a note on nested replacement text in modifying acts.

**Anchor status:** No duplicate anchors exist. The nested heading carries `{#art-1}` (representing the rewritten art. 1 of HG 273/1994); the first article of the Regulament (Anexă) carries `{#art-1-b}` per the existing duplicate-suffix convention. `validate-citation-anchors.mjs` reports 44 anchors, no duplicates — this is correct.

**Parser/RAG guidance:** Consumers should use `article_count` from metadata (43) as the authoritative semantic count, not the raw heading count from the Markdown file.
